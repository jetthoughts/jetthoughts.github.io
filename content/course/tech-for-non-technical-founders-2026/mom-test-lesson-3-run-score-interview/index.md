> **Micro-Lesson 3 of 3 · Module 2** · [From Idea to First Paying Customer](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/)
>
> **Input:** your 5-8 question list (from Lesson 1) + the 3 emotional flags (from Lesson 2)
>
> **Output:** a scored interview transcript with a 1-10 rating and a strong/weak/fail verdict

---

**1. Hook (3 sentences):**

You have the script and the flags - the worst thing you can do now is improvise mid-call ("oh that reminds me of my product idea") and contaminate the rest of the transcript with your pitch. In this lesson you'll run a real 30-minute interview, score it within 5 minutes of hanging up, and decide whether the signal is strong enough to act on.

**2. One-sentence outcome:**

After this lesson you will be able to: **run a 30-minute Mom Test interview following the 5-question script in order, score the call 1-10 within 5 minutes of hanging up, and classify it as strong signal, polite default, or no problem.**

**3. Minimal concept block (300 words):**

**The interview flow (30 minutes, no deviations):**

1. Open the call. Script on your second screen. Thank them, then go straight to Q1.
2. Ask Q1-Q5 in order. Do not skip. Do not rephrase. The wording is load-bearing.
3. Listen for the 3 emotional flags (Lesson 2) while they talk. Take notes by hand - handwriting forces you to listen, not transcribe.
4. End the call. Thank them. Hang up. Score immediately.

**The 1-10 scoring rubric (score within 5 minutes):**

Combine two inputs: the Q4 self-rating and your emotional flag count.

| Score | Q4 number | Flag count | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **8-10** | 7+ with comparison | 3+ flags | Strong signal - real felt problem |
| **5-7** | 5-6, or bare 7 with no comparison | 2 flags | Polite default - ambiguous, treat as a 5 |
| **0-4** | Below 5 | 0-1 flags | No felt problem - discount the call |

**The scoring rule that prevents wishful thinking:** if you score later than 5 minutes after the call, you will round up. Your gut in the moment is more honest than your brain after a week of wanting the number to be higher. Score it, write it down, lock it in.

**The build/pivot/kill threshold (after all 10 interviews):**

- **Build:** 7+ of 10 scored 7 or higher, with 3+ flags each → problem is validated. Proceed to prototype.
- **Pivot:** 4-6 of 10 scored 7+ → problem is real for some, not all. Tighten the ICP and run 5 more.
- **Kill:** 3 or fewer scored 7+ → problem isn't acute enough to build for. Go back to [Lesson 1.1](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/form-your-founding-hypothesis-90-minute-sprint/) and rewrite the hypothesis.

**5. Do this now (5 steps, success check on step 5):**

1. Print your question list from Lesson 1. Keep it open on a second screen or a physical sheet of paper.
2. Find a friend, a co-founder, or anyone willing to be a practice interviewee. Tell them: "I'm practicing customer interviews. Talk to me about [problem area] for 20 minutes."
3. Run Q1-Q5 in order. Take notes by hand. Do not pitch your product. If they ask what you're building, say "I'll tell you after - right now I want to hear about your experience."
4. Hang up. Start a 5-minute timer. Write down: their Q4 number, your flag count (frustration, workaround, urgency), and a 1-10 score using the rubric above.
5. **Success check:** You have three numbers written down - Q4 self-rating, flag count, final 1-10 score. All three were written within 5 minutes of hanging up. If you forgot to count flags, re-listen to the call at 1.5× speed and count them now.

**6. Error recovery (Minimalist Instruction):**

**If this fails:** Halfway through Q2 you pitched your product - "oh that sounds like what we're building!" - and for the rest of the call the interviewee nodded along instead of describing their life. **Why:** when someone describes your exact problem, the instinct to validate your idea overrides the discipline to stay silent. This is the most common first-timer failure. **Fix:** discard the call. The transcript is contaminated - the interviewee is now evaluating your product, not their own experience. Run the next practice interview with these two rules taped to your monitor: (1) "I'll tell you about it after the call," (2) "Right now I'm just here to learn."

**7. Micro-reflection (1 question):**

- What was your practice interviewee's 1-10 score? Based on the build/pivot/kill threshold, if all 10 interviews scored like this one, would you build, pivot, or kill?

**8. Bridge to next lesson:**

Next: you'll recruit 10 real interviewees who match your ICP - not friends, not other founders, but actual people who have the problem your hypothesis names. Your script is ready. Your flag detector is calibrated. Now you need names.

> **Done when:** You've run one practice interview, scored it 1-10 within 5 minutes, and locked in the three numbers (Q4, flags, final score) in your `Founder OS` folder.
> **Next click:** [2.3a · Find 10 People With the Problem](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/find-10-people-where-to-look/)
> **If blocked:** If you can't find a practice interviewee, interview yourself. Open a voice memo app, read Q1 aloud, then answer it out loud as if you were the ICP. Score your own answers. If you can't give yourself a 7+, your hypothesis problem might not be acute enough - even for you. That's a signal worth paying attention to.

> **Case Study: Tomas & Mia**
>
> **Tomas** (B2B SaaS): Ran 10 interviews with accounting firm controllers. 7 scored 8/10 with 3+ flags each. Q2 consistently hit - "My CFO bills $200/hr and spent 4 hours on reconciliation last week." Verdict: BUILD. Proceeded to clickable prototype validation.
>
> **Mia** (B2C marketplace): Ran 10 interviews with parents from Facebook groups. 8 scored 9/10. Q3 surfaced the real pain - "I missed a $2,000 client deadline because I was on hold with a tutoring center." Verdict: BUILD. Strongly validated.
