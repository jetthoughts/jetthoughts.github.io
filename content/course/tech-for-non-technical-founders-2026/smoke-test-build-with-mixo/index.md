You finished [Lesson 1.1](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/form-your-founding-hypothesis-90-minute-sprint/) with a hypothesis sentence. To find out if anyone wants what it describes, you need that hypothesis live as a URL where strangers can sign up. The format is called a **smoke test page** - one page that describes what you would build and asks for an email if the visitor wants in.

**[Mixo](https://www.mixo.io/)** (free AI page builder, no credit card) drafts the page from a one-line prompt. You then tighten the copy, swap the placeholder image, and publish - ~45 minutes start to finish.

## Do this now

1. **Sign up at [mixo.io](https://www.mixo.io/)** (free, email only). Paste your Founding Hypothesis sentence into the "Describe your idea" box. Click **Generate**.

2. **Wait ~60 seconds.** Mixo produces a draft page with a top-of-page headline, a sub-headline below it, a placeholder hero image, a section of 3-4 value props, an email signup form, and a footer.

3. **Tighten the headline.** Click it in Mixo's editor. It must name a specific person + a specific outcome - "Solo chiropractors: resubmit denied claims in 30 seconds," NOT "Smart Solutions for Modern Businesses." If Mixo's headline is generic, edit it directly or re-prompt with 5-10 more specific words from your hypothesis.

4. **Tighten the sub-headline.** It must explain HOW the outcome happens, in one line. The mechanism, not more marketing.

5. **Tighten the 3-4 value props.** Rewrite each as an outcome the customer wants ("Stop calling 8 tutoring centers" / "Book a parent-reviewed tutor in 2 clicks"), NOT as feature names ("Calendar integration" / "Stripe-powered payments").

6. **Tighten the action button + form.** Mixo may give you "Sign up" or "Buy now." Change the button label to "Get on the waitlist." Confirm there is an email field below it. Do NOT use a "Buy now" button - you cannot legally sell what does not exist yet.

7. **Swap the placeholder hero image.** Click it. The image must show your customer's **current pain** (a parent on the phone at 9pm, a stack of denied insurance claims on a desk), NOT your unbuilt product (a polished product screenshot you have not built breaks visitor trust). Short on time? Delete the image and let the headline carry the page.

8. **Add a "Coming soon" line in the footer.** Mixo will NOT add this by default. In Mixo's footer section, type: "Coming soon. Email signup reserves your spot at launch." This keeps you honest about what visitors are signing up for.

9. **Strip the extras Mixo included by default.** If Mixo added a Pricing table, FAQ, Testimonials, or "How it works" section that was not in your hypothesis, delete it. Each extra section dilutes the path from headline → email signup.

10. **Publish + verify it loads.** Click Mixo's **Publish** button (top right). Mixo gives you a free URL like `yourname.mixo.io`. Open it in an **incognito window** (Chrome: `Cmd/Ctrl+Shift+N` - what visitors see, not your editor) to confirm the page actually published and looks right.

11. **Test on one real stranger.** Send your URL to ONE real person who has not seen your work - any stranger; they do NOT need to be your target customer (this tests headline clarity, not buying interest). Ask them: "In 3 seconds, who is this page for and what does it do?" If they can answer both, your headline clearly names your ICP and outcome - lesson done. If they cannot, the headline is almost always the fix; rewrite it and retest.

## If this fails

- **Strangers cannot name who the page is for or what it does, even after 2 headline rewrites.** Your hypothesis `[customer]` or `[differentiation]` blank is still too vague. Back to [Lesson 1.1](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/form-your-founding-hypothesis-90-minute-sprint/) and tighten ("solo chiropractors," not "small businesses").

- **Mixo's draft reads generic after 2 regenerations.** Your hypothesis `[problem]` blank is too vague. Same fix - back to [Lesson 1.1](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/form-your-founding-hypothesis-90-minute-sprint/).

- **Mixo does not fit your idea, or you want template control.** Fall back to **[Carrd](https://carrd.co/)** (no-code drag-drop, free for 3 sites). Use the same 11-step workflow but write each copy block yourself first - prompt Claude/ChatGPT with this, paste the output into Carrd:

  ```text
  Translate this hypothesis into 6 landing-page elements
  (headline, sub-headline, 3 value props, CTA copy, footer disclaimer):
  [PASTE HYPOTHESIS]
  ```
