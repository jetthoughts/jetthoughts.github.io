> **Micro-Lesson Example · Module 5** · [From Idea to First Paying Customer](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/)
>
> **Input:** 1-2 warm leads who booked demos from Chapter 5.3
>
> **Output:** one signed DPA with a cleared Stripe deposit before any new code ships

---

**1. Hook (3 sentences):**

Six weeks of free build, every Friday demo "this is great" - and the year-one proposal thread was dead by Wednesday because the CFO never approved the pilot. That CFO question should have landed on Week 0. In this lesson you'll make it land on Week 0, with a one-page agreement and a refundable deposit that separates real demand from polite enthusiasm.

**2. One-sentence outcome:**

After this lesson you will be able to: **prepare a one-page Design Partner Agreement with the right deposit amount and a Stripe payment link, ready to send to a warm lead.**

**3. Minimal concept block (250 words):**

The Design Partner Agreement (DPA) is a one-page mutual LOI (letter of intent - a short agreement before a full contract). Six sections. One page. No lawyer needed for v1. The structure:

| Section | What goes in it |
|---|---|
| 1. Scope | 3 outcomes + 2 use cases. Everything else out of scope. |
| 2. Duration | 6-8 weeks, named end date, weekly Friday demo at a fixed time. |
| **3. Deposit** (load-bearing) | 10-30% of year-one ACV (annual contract value - what one customer pays in a year), paid via Stripe before kickoff, credited toward year-one on conversion. |
| 4. Success criteria | 3 measurable outcomes. 2 of 3 hit → auto-convert. |
| 5. Conversion terms | Year-one price in dollars, billing cadence, 30-day notice. |
| 6. Data + IP | Customer keeps data. You keep product IP. 30-day exit. |

**The deposit bands (pick your row):**

- **B2B SaaS (per-seat, 5-10 seats):** $500-$3K deposit on $5K-$12K ACV. CFO approves on email.
- **B2B Services / consultancy:** $1K-$6K deposit on $10K-$40K ACV. Customer expects to pay.

**The $500 floor:** Below $500 the deposit stops working as a commitment device - the customer can write it off as an impulse purchase and ghost. For low-ACV B2B SaaS where 10% falls under $500, charge the first month's revenue upfront instead (e.g., $97/mo → $97 deposit).

**The credit rule:** The deposit is not separate revenue. It is "year-one ACV, pre-paid." The customer's CFO is approving year-one money brought forward, not a discretionary line item. Naming it correctly changes the entire conversation.

**5. Do this now (5 steps, success check on step 5):**

1. Open a Google Doc. Copy the DPA template from the [full chapter](/course/tech-for-non-technical-founders-2026/paid-pilot-charge-before-ship/#dpa-template). The verbatim block is already formatted - paste it as-is.
2. Fill the 6 bracketed sections. Write the 3 success criteria in the customer's own words from your demo conversation, not your product language.
3. Pick your deposit number from the bands above. If unsure between two bands, pick the midpoint of the smaller band.
4. Set up Stripe: go to [stripe.com/login](https://dashboard.stripe.com/login) → Products → Create product "Design Partner Pilot" → one-time price for your deposit → generate Payment Link. Copy the URL. Five minutes, no code.
5. Send the DPA (PDF) + Stripe link to your warmest lead from Chapter 5.3 who said "I'd love to try this." **Success check:** Email sent within the hour. The Stripe link is live. The DPA has all brackets filled - no blanks, no "TBD."

**6. Error recovery (Minimalist Instruction):**

**If this fails:** The customer says "can we start free and convert later?" **Why:** the deposit threatens their commitment - they're testing whether you'll soften. Free pilots always lose CFO approval in Week 8 because the CFO never approved Week 0. **Fix:** reframe: "The deposit is year-one ACV prepaid, not added cost. $500 lands in this month's books and forces the CFO to approve the line item now - not discover it in Week 8." If they still say no, they are not in your must-have segment. Move to the next lead.

**7. Micro-reflection (2 questions):**

- What is your deposit number, and which warm lead from Chapter 5.3 gets the email first?
- Save your filled DPA as a PDF in your `Founder OS` folder. If you close this pilot, this PDF is Artifact #6 of 6 - the one investors fund.

> **Case Study: Tomas & Mia**
>
> **Tomas** (B2B SaaS): Signs a DPA with 3 accounting firms. Deposit: $2,500 each, refundable. 2 of 3 sign and pay within 48 hours. Revenue: $5,000 in committed deposits. The deposit amount was 25% of $10K year-one ACV - right in the B2B SaaS band.
>
> **Mia** (B2C marketplace): Signs a DPA with 4 parents. Deposit: $50 each (2× the $25/mo tutoring fee). The $500 B2B floor doesn't apply - consumer buyers sign without a CFO. 4 of 4 sign and pay. Revenue: $200. Lower deposit, higher volume - consumer math.
