Getting from idea to a product someone pays for is harder when you can't read the code. This course gives you the tools to do it anyway - hiring, managing, and if needed, fixing what the first team broke.

Free. No signup.

## Why this course exists

Five mistakes keep showing up in JetThoughts rescue calls:

1. Hiring a dev shop before validating the problem.
2. Paying for six months of "building" with no way to see what shipped.
3. Signing an SOW that defines "delivered" as "deployed to staging" - then watching $78K clear before the staging URL works.
4. Rebuilding from scratch when salvage would have closed the gap in eight weeks.
5. Shipping an AI feature only to discover the agency was passing $4,800 of OpenAI tokens through the invoice.

Reading this course should lower your odds of being our next rescue client. That is the point.

## Take this course if

- You have an idea, a half-built MVP, or a dev shop you no longer trust
- You can read a Stripe invoice but not a GitHub commit
- You've been told "the architecture is fine" and have no way to check
- You want to know what to ask, not what to build
- You're considering firing your current team and don't know what comes next
- You write code yourself but want a structured pass on the validation stage (Modules 1-4) before you start typing

## This is not for you if

- You want to learn to code
- You want to outsource founder judgment

## What you need to start

An idea, or a half-built MVP. The course shows you how to validate it cheaply, find the version with real demand, and confirm there's a business before you commit money to building.

## What you walk away with

A personal **Founder OS** - six filled-in artifacts that document your route from idea to first paying users:

1. A one-sentence **Founding Hypothesis** (Module 1)
2. A landing-page **smoke test decision** + price point (Module 2)
3. Ten **Mom Test interview notes** + prototype feedback (Module 3)
4. A one-page **Product Brief / Vibe PRD** (Module 4)
5. A **live MVP** at a real URL, ownership locked Day 1 (Module 5)
6. A **first signed paid pilot** + channel sequence running (Module 6)

You can hand any of these to an investor or a co-founder and answer their questions from the artifacts alone.

## If Your Team Is Already Failing

If you're reading this in the middle of a stalled build or a runaway invoice, the rest of the course can wait. Run these 6 steps now.

1. Check who owns GitHub, AWS, database, and domain. If it's not you, fix that first. - [Ownership Audit](/blog/github-aws-database-ownership-checklist/)
2. Demand one Friday demo with working software only - no Jira, no slides, no "almost done." - [Friday Demo Rule](/blog/friday-demo-rule-founder-progress/)
3. Require a plain-English weekly report: what shipped, what's blocked, what's next. - [Weekly Dev Report](/blog/weekly-dev-report-template-founders/)
4. Score salvage vs rebuild and plan your team transition before you authorize any more work. - [Salvage vs Rebuild and Team Transition Reference](/blog/hire-track-supplementary-reference/)
5. If your team is shipping AI features, run the 5-question agency audit before approving more work. - ["We Use AI" Follow-Up Questions](/blog/agency-uses-ai-follow-up-questions/)
