Got a Slack from a founder Tuesday saying her team kept telling her "next week" and she'd run out of follow-ups, so we hopped on a quick call. Three sprints in a row had ended the same way - is it on track? yes. When will it be ready? next week. Then next week would show up and the whole thing would start over. When I asked her what either of those questions had ever told her, she didn't have an answer.

So we agreed she'd try a different one this week. Instead of yes-or-no, she'd ask the team for the smallest thing they could actually put in front of her by Friday - even one screen with new data on it. Either she gets something to look at, or she finds out the team can't produce anything specific. Beats three more sprints of "is it on track?"

Anyone else stuck asking questions that don't pay back?
