# LinkedIn ICP-E Validation Plan

**Purpose:** Validate whether LinkedIn can surface and qualify ICP-E: non-technical founders who feel stuck with a dev shop, freelancer, offshore team, or AI-heavy build they cannot evaluate.
**Window:** 2 weeks
**Cadence:** 5 posts/week, 10 posts total
**Primary CTA:** ask for a practical artifact (`DEMO`, `ACCESS`, `REPORT`, `REPO`, `TRANSFER`) or reply with one concrete symptom. No calendar-link CTA in the post body.
**Source docs:** `docs/90-99-content-strategy/strategy-analysis/90.10-icp-primary-website-target.md`, `docs/projects/2510-seo-content-strategy/20-29-strategy/20.07-content-plan-icp-e-q2-2026.md`

---

## Validation Question

Can we find founders who are not yet searching Google for "fire my dev shop," but already feel the earlier control-loss symptoms?

The ICP is validated if LinkedIn comments and DMs show the same language as the ICP doc:

- "I can't tell if they're making progress."
- "Jira says everything is in progress, but I can't click anything."
- "The repo/cloud/accounts are under their name."
- "I asked for something simple and got an overbuilt mess."
- "The cheap developer was fine until the product needed senior judgment."

---

## Hypotheses

| ID | Hypothesis | Signal That Confirms It |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Control-before-rescue content will outperform rescue-after-disaster content. | Posts about demos, access, ownership, and progress verification get more founder comments/DMs than "fire your dev shop" posts. |
| H2 | Founders respond to concrete scripts and checklists more than opinions. | Save rate, reposts, and "send me this" comments are highest on artifact posts. |
| H3 | Founder pain language beats technical accuracy language. | Posts using "I can't tell if they're working" outperform posts about code quality, architecture, or AI code debt. |
| H4 | LinkedIn can reveal new ICP sub-segments. | Comments identify repeated roles beyond founder/CEO: COO, product lead, investor, operator, or solo founder using AI tools. |
| H5 | Artifact CTA beats calendar-link CTA. | "Comment DEMO" and "Comment ACCESS" get more qualified replies than direct "book a call" CTAs. |

## Audience Filters

Use these filters while posting, commenting, and reviewing responders:

| Segment | Include | Exclude |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Founder/CEO, non-technical or business-led, 1-20 employees, pre-seed to Series A | Senior engineers debating implementation details |
| Secondary | COO, Head of Product, investor/operator supporting portfolio companies | Agency owners selling services |
| Pain State | Current vendor opacity, missing access, unclear progress, late delivery, surprise costs | Generic hiring curiosity |
| Budget Signal | Has paid a team already, raised money, or has active product revenue | Wants free technical mentoring only |

## 2-Week Posting Plan

### Week 1 - Progress Visibility

| Day | Post Hook | Format | ICP Test | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | "Jira is not progress. A founder needs one clickable thing every Friday." | Founder POV post | Do founders recognize progress mirage? | Comment `DEMO` for the Friday demo script |
| Tue | "If your dev team can't show staging this week, ask this exact question." | Tactical script | Do scripts get saves/replies? | Comment `QUESTION` for the exact wording |
| Wed | Poll: "What makes you trust a dev team is actually working?" | Poll | Which proof matters most? | Vote + explain what your team sends now |
| Thu | "A status report with 12 completed tickets can still mean zero product progress." | Story post | Does ticket skepticism resonate? | Comment `REPORT` for the report sanity check |
| Fri | "The Friday Demo Rule: 5 things non-technical founders should see." | Checklist carousel | Do checklists earn qualified comments? | Comment `DEMO` |

### Week 2 - Ownership & Access

| Day | Post Hook | Format | ICP Test | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | "You don't own your product if your vendor owns GitHub, AWS, and the database." | Blunt POV | Does hostage-language hit? | Comment `ACCESS` for the ownership checklist |
| Tue | "Ask your dev shop who owns these 7 accounts before you pay the next invoice." | Checklist | Which access risks surprise founders? | Comment `ACCOUNTS` for the list |
| Wed | Poll: "Which account would hurt most to lose?" | Poll | GitHub vs cloud vs domain vs database priority | Vote + comment with the one you do not control |
| Thu | "Read access to GitHub is not ownership." | Short teardown | Does legal/control framing create DMs? | Comment `REPO` for the repo access test |
| Fri | "The vendor transition checklist I wish every founder had before signing." | Carousel | Do founders want a transition artifact? | Comment `TRANSFER` |

## Engagement Protocol

1. Reply to founder comments within 2 hours.
2. Ask one clarifying question before offering help.
3. Move to DM only after sending the requested artifact or when the person shares a real symptom.
4. Tag every qualified interaction with one pain label: `progress`, `access`, `requirements`, `cheap-talent`, `salvage`.
5. Save exact founder phrases for ICP updates and blog hooks.

Example reply:

> "When you say 'progress,' do you mean tickets are moving, or you can actually click through the feature on staging?"

## Measurement

### Quantitative

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---:|---|
| Average engagement rate | 4-6% | Matches B2B LinkedIn benchmark in tracking docs |
| Qualified comments | 3+/week | Public proof the pain language lands |
| Qualified DMs | 2+/week | Stronger buying-intent signal after artifact request |
| Saves | 10+/artifact post | Checklist/template utility |
| Profile views from ICP roles | 20+/week | Audience fit |
| Website clicks with UTM | 10+/week | Movement from social to owned site |
| Discovery calls attributed to LinkedIn | 1+/month | Commercial validation |

### Qualitative

Track these after each post:

| Question | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Did responders self-identify as non-technical? | "I'm not technical," "I can't read code," "I'm the business founder" |
| Did they mention a current vendor/team? | Agency, freelancer, offshore team, AI builder, internal junior dev |
| Did they describe control loss? | Missing access, unclear progress, no staging, vague reports |
| Did they ask for an artifact? | Checklist, script, template, audit rubric |
| Did they share urgency? | Runway, investor update, launch date, churn, delayed users |

Decision rules:

- If one pillar produces 50%+ of qualified comments/DMs, move the next blog post toward that pillar.
- If a post gets high likes but no founder comments, treat it as awareness, not ICP validation.
- If technical people argue in comments but founders stay quiet, rewrite the hook in founder pain language.
- If a CTA gets artifact requests, turn that artifact into a lead magnet.

## ICP Update Rules

Update `90.10-icp-primary-website-target.md` only when the same signal appears at least 3 times from qualified responders.

Add evidence under one of these sections:

- Pain language
- Triggers
- Objections
- Control-loss patterns
- New sub-segments
- Lead magnet ideas

Do not update the ICP from likes, generic praise, or agency-owner comments.

## Blog Plan Feedback Loop

After 2 weeks:

| LinkedIn Winner | Blog Plan Action |
|---|---|
| Friday Demo Rule wins | Prioritize `friday-demo-rule-founder-progress` |
| Access/ownership wins | Prioritize `github-aws-database-ownership-checklist` |
| Artifact requests beat comments | Build the requested artifact before writing another post |
| Neither pillar wins | Run the backlog hooks for one more week before changing ICP |

If none win, the ICP may still be right but LinkedIn packaging is wrong. Test founder-specific distribution next: founder groups, investor/operator comments, and direct founder outreach using the same hooks.
