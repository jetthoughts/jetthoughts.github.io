> *When my devshop delivers late, over-budget, and buggy code that I can't evaluate myself, I want a transparent partner who explains everything in plain English and holds themselves accountable — so I can stop worrying about development and focus on growing my business.*

## 4. What Exceeds Expectations

1. **Weekly reports in plain English** — code health score, bug count, test coverage explained simply
2. **Clear SOW with deliverables, timelines, milestones** — knows exactly what they're paying for
3. **Code ownership guaranteed after every milestone** — legal guarantee in contract
4. **Dedicated point of contact** — one person who translates tech to business
5. **Structured onboarding with documentation** — Day 1 plan, access setup, PM tool setup
6. **QA reports in understandable format** — "Your app has 12 bugs, 8 critical. Here's the fix plan."
7. **Termination clause for quality issues and delays** — "If we miss 2 milestones, you can walk."
8. **Proactive problem-flagging** — "We found a potential issue — here are 3 options."

## 11. Content Tone Rules (for agents)

When writing for ICP-E audience:
- **Plain English only** — zero jargon. If you must use a technical term, explain it in parentheses.
- **Empathy first** — acknowledge the pain before offering the solution. They've been burned.
- **Specific over vague** — "$12K planned, $12.8K actual (+6.7%)" beats "on budget."
- **Founder voice** — write like a founder talking to a founder, not an enterprise vendor.
- **No generic AI language** — "unlock", "harness", "leverage" are banned.
- **Show, don't claim** — sample reports, sample SOWs, case studies > adjectives.
