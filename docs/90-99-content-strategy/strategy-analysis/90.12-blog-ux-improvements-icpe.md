# Blog UX Improvements for ICP-E Readers

**Source:** Founder persona UX review (2026-04-11)
**Status:** Actionable recommendations — implement incrementally

---

## Context

A non-technical founder (Series A, $1.2M raised, crashing app) browsed 9 technical blog posts at 11pm on their phone. The review revealed that while the technical content is strong, the blog's UX doesn't serve founders who are evaluating JetThoughts as a partner.

**Key insight:** Founders don't read tech posts for the tech. They read them to answer: "Can I trust this company with my product?"

---

## 5 UX Improvements (prioritized)

### 1. Add "Who this is for" line under every post title

One sentence, right after the date. Costs nothing. Saves wrong readers 90 seconds.

```markdown
**For:** Rails developers managing production performance
```
or
```markdown
**For:** Non-technical founders evaluating their dev team
```

**Implementation:** Add a `audience:` frontmatter field. Render in the post template below the date.

### 2. Add founder/developer filter labels in blog listing

Right now every post looks identical in the blog index. A tag label — "For Founders" vs "For Developers" — lets non-technical readers self-select.

Posts that belong in the founder track:
- fire-dev-shop-guide
- async-remote-xp-practices
- django-technical-debt-cost-calculator-elimination-strategy
- code-quality-evaluation-non-technical-founders
- remote-team-accountability-non-technical-founders
- fractional-cto-roi-calculator-startup-decision-framework
- All "hiring" and "outsourcing" posts

**Implementation:** Use categories ("Startup" vs "Engineering") or a custom `audience` taxonomy in Hugo.

### 3. Business consequence in first sentence of every tech post

The formula: **problem in business terms first, technical explanation second.**

| Post | Current opening | Better opening |
|---|---|---|
| Propshaft vs Sprockets | "HTTP/2 multiplexing and import maps..." | "Your asset pipeline is slowing every page load by 200ms. Here's what Rails 8 ships instead." |
| Hotwire Turbo 8 | "Turbo 8 introduces morphing..." | "Your users see a page reload every click. With Turbo 8 morphing, they don't." |
| YJIT Performance | "YJIT achieves 94.7% speedup..." | "A free configuration change makes your Rails app 20-50% faster. No code rewrites." |

### 4. Explain why a Rails company writes about Django/Laravel

Founders see Django, Laravel, and Rails posts mixed together and wonder: "Is this actually a Rails company?"

**Fix:** One line in the blog header or about section:
> "We specialize in Ruby on Rails. We write about the full web stack because good engineering principles cross frameworks."

Or: tag non-Rails posts with "Cross-Framework" category.

### 5. Add "What this means for your business" callout box on tech posts

2-3 sentences at the bottom of every deep-technical post. This is what a founder screenshots and sends to their advisor at 11pm.

```markdown
> **Not technical?** In plain terms: a free configuration change that makes
> your app respond 20-50% faster with no code rewrites. Ask your dev team
> if YJIT is enabled in production — if not, it's a 5-minute fix.
```

**Implementation:** Add a `business_summary:` frontmatter field. Render in the post template as a styled callout box before "Further Reading."
