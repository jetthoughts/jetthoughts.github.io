## MODULE 1: Hypothesis & Smoke Test (2-3 weeks)

### 1.1 · Form Your Founding Hypothesis
- **⏳ Wait:** None
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** Rough idea or instinct
- **🔑 Setup:** Google account (for `Founder OS` folder in Google Drive)
- **📝 Template:** Mad Libs sentence — 5 blanks: `[customer]`, `[problem]`, `[approach]`, `[competition]`, `[differentiation]`
- **📝 Quality gate:** Score ≥14/20 across 4 lenses (Customer, Pragmatic, Growth, Money); no lens below 2
- **📦 Output:** One-sentence Founding Hypothesis saved in `Founder OS` Google Drive folder
- **🆘 Plan B — can't fill blanks:** Copy-paste 2-3 verbatim Reddit/G2 customer complaints via Perplexity search (prompt in 1.2). Replace `[problem]` blank with their exact words. Leave blanks as placeholders if no quotes yet — complete after Module 2 interviews.
- **🆘 Plan B — score below 14 or lens at 1:** Find a verbatim quote from a real person. The fastest route is the Perplexity research prompt in 1.2. Three reads aloud to a stranger — the blank they trip on gets tightened first.
- **🆘 Plan B — every blank is specific but sentence sounds generic:** Read aloud to one stranger. If they say "wait, can you say that again," rewrite the blank they tripped on.

### 1.2 · Build Smoke-Test Page
- **⏳ Wait:** None
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Mixo/Carrd free tier)
- **📦 Input:** Founding Hypothesis sentence (1.1)
- **🔑 Setup:** Mixo account (email signup, ~60 seconds)
- **📝 Template:** Step 2 builder prompt — fill `[CUSTOMER]`, `[PROBLEM]`, `[COMPETITION]`, `[DIFFERENTIATION]` blanks + 2-3 `[verbatim customer quote]` lines
- **📝 Pre-work:** Perplexity search for 5-8 verbatim customer complaints from Reddit/G2 (10 min). Keep source URLs — Lesson 2.3 reads these same threads for people-hunting.
- **📝 Success check:** One stranger names who the page is for and what it does in 3 seconds
- **📦 Output:** Live landing page URL (e.g., `yourname.mixo.io`)
- **🆘 Plan B — strangers can't name it after 2 headline rewrites:** `[customer]` or `[differentiation]` blank is too vague → back to 1.1, tighten to a specific noun. Solo chiropractors, not small businesses.
- **🆘 Plan B — builder reads generic after 2 regenerations:** `[problem]` blank is too vague → back to 1.1, tighten to one specific failure mode.
- **🆘 Plan B — no AI builder fits your idea:** Drop to Carrd (no-code drag-drop). Same workflow, same Step 2 prompt pasted into Carrd's AI assistant.

### 1.3 · Wire Tracking
- **⏳ Wait:** None
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (both tools free)
- **📦 Input:** Live landing page URL (1.2)
- **🔑 Setup:** Microsoft account (for Clarity) + Google account (for GA4). Most people already have both — reuse them.
- **📝 Action:** Paste 2 tracking snippets into page builder's head-tag field (Clarity + GA4). Verify in incognito window + submit test form.
- **📦 Output:** Clarity session recording verified + GA4 test visit confirmed
- **🆘 Plan B — Clarity shows "No data yet" after 5 min:** Snippet is in wrong field (body instead of head tag) or builder preview is blocking scripts. Move to head-tag field, publish, wait 1 hour, re-check.
- **🆘 Plan B — GA4 shows no test visit after 5 min:** Same cause. Move to head-tag, publish page, refresh GA4 Realtime.
- **🆘 Plan B — only one "custom code" field:** That field IS the head-tag. Paste all snippets there.

### 1.4 · Run Smoke Test (300 Cold Visitors)
- **⏳ Wait:** Ad-account approval 24-72 hours. Meta slowest; Reddit same-day. 7-day traffic run.
- **💰 Cost:** Dependent on channel:
  - Meta: $250-$700 ($0.70-$1.90 CPC × 300 + 20% slack)
  - LinkedIn: $1,650-$6,600 ($5.70-$22 CPC)
  - Google Search: $900-$2,400 ($3-$8 CPC)
  - Reddit: $375-$900 ($1.25-$3 CPC)
  - **$0 path:** Organic sharing via smoke-test channel guide (slower but real)
- **📦 Input:** Landing page with Clarity + GA4 installed (1.3)
- **🔑 Setup:** Ad platform account (Meta/Google/LinkedIn/Reddit) + payment method + ad account approval. Install channel's pixel (Meta/LinkedIn/Reddit) in head-tag. Google Ads users: skip pixel — GA4 links directly.
- **📝 Action:** Create campaign, set daily budget to reach 300 visits in 5-7 days, do NOT touch the page for the full run
- **📦 Output:** Conversion rate (form submits ÷ campaign landing-page views) + go/iterate/kill decision
- **🆘 Plan B — conversion <3%:** Hypothesis is wrong. Go back to 1.1, tighten the blank that scored lowest on the 4-lens test.
- **🆘 Plan B — conversion >20%:** Either hot market (rare) or ad targeting too narrow (common). Verify with a second channel. LinkedIn 22% + Reddit 3% = targeting issue; both near the rate = real.
- **🆘 Plan B — ad budget out of reach:** Use $0 organic path in smoke-test channel guide.

### 1.5 · Price Hypothesis
- **⏳ Wait:** Stripe verification 1-3 business days. Bank account + tax ID required. Sometimes requests ID upload. **Start the weekend before launch.**
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Stripe free to set up; per-transaction fees on actual payments)
- **📦 Input:** Live landing page from 1.4
- **🔑 Setup:** Stripe account at stripe.com — requires bank account + tax ID (SSN for US sole proprietors). Stripe doesn't verify incorporation at signup.
- **📝 Template:** Default price anchor: price against time saved. Find 2-3 existing tools in category, start at their middle tier. One-time (not subscription) for founding member conversion.
- **📝 CTA copy:** Pick ONE framing — outcome ("Stop spending 4 hours on reconciliation - $97") OR risk-reduction ("Reserve your spot - $97 refundable for 30 days"). Do not A/B test.
- **📦 Output:** Measured click-to-payment rate from Stripe Payment Link
- **🆘 Plan B — Stripe verification takes >3 days:** Build page without the button. Run email-only smoke test from 1.4 while Stripe processes. Demand signal doesn't depend on price button being live today.
- **🆘 Plan B — visitors click button but nobody completes payment:** Track both click (page→Stripe) and completion (Stripe→thank-you). 60 clicks with 3 completions = checkout killing intent. 6 clicks with 3 completions = half of clickers bought — promising but too small a sample; keep running until 20+ clicks.
- **🆘 Plan B — not ready for Stripe at all:** Use the Fake-Stripe Pre-Sale variant ($1 refundable charge, Pieter Levels style) as a lower-friction alternative.

## MODULE 2: Validate the Problem (3-5 weeks)

### 2.1 · The Mom Test: Ask About the Past
- **⏳ Wait:** None
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** Founding Hypothesis + `[customer]` blank (ICP description from 1.1)
- **🔑 Setup:** None. Google Doc in existing `Founder OS` folder.
- **📝 Template:** 5 verbatim rewrite questions from the Mom Test table + 2-3 ICP-specific probes. Every question anchored in a past event, none asking "would you."
- **📦 Output:** 5-8 question list in `Mom Test draft - [date]` Google Doc
- **🆘 Plan B — questions still sound hypothetical:** Rewrite to start with "Tell me about the last time..." or "Walk me through what you did when...". If you can't anchor it to a past moment, cut it.
- **🆘 Plan B — stuck on question design:** Open Mom Test Interview Script — it has the 5 questions verbatim with follow-ups.

### 2.2 · AI Persona Rehearsal (OPTIONAL)
- **⏳ Wait:** None
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Claude/ChatGPT free tier)
- **📦 Input:** Draft question list from 2.1
- **🔑 Setup:** Claude or ChatGPT account (free tier)
- **📝 Action:** Build 3 ICP personas, test each question in-character, surface 3 objections
- **📦 Output:** Sharpened question list + Objection Tracker
- **🆘 Plan B — skip entirely:** If you've run customer interviews before and your questions reliably produce concrete past-tense answers, skip to 2.3.

### 2.3 · Find 10 People: Where to Look
- **⏳ Wait:** None (research step)
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** `[customer]` blank from 1.1 + Reddit/G2 source URLs from 1.2 research
- **🔑 Setup:** None. Reddit, LinkedIn, X — no special accounts needed.
- **📝 Template:** AI ICP map prompt (paste into Claude). Generate 8 communities + 5 search strings from hypothesis.
- **📦 Output:** 30-name list in a spreadsheet
- **🆘 Plan B — can't find anyone:** Hypothesis `[customer]` is too vague. Tighten to "12-person law-firm office manager" not "small business owners." Search second-degree keywords ("boarding costs" not "pet sitter").
- **🆘 Plan B — community is gated/private:** Use research-panel fallback (UserInterviews, Respondent, or Askable) — pay $25-$50 per screener.

### 2.4 · Find 10 People: What to Say
- **⏳ Wait:** 2-4 calendar weeks to book 10 interviews (the long pole of Module 2)
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (organic outreach). $250-$500 if using research panel fallback.
- **📦 Input:** 30-name list from 2.3
- **🔑 Setup:** LinkedIn account (for DMs) or email. Calendly free tier for scheduling (optional).
- **📝 Template:** 3-message outreach sequence from Outreach Sequence Template
- **📦 Output:** 10 booked interviews on the calendar
- **🆘 Plan B — reply rate under 15% after 30 messages:** Expand the list to 60 names using the AI prompt from 2.3. Usually an ICP-fit problem — people you messaged aren't in enough pain.
- **🆘 Plan B — need interviews fast, organic is too slow:** Research panel (UserInterviews at ~$50/screener, Respondent, Askable). 10 interviews × $50 = $500.

### [Run 10 Mom Test Interviews — Days, Not One Sitting]
- **⏳ Wait:** Days to weeks depending on scheduling
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** 2.1 script + 2.4 booked interviewees
- **🆘 Plan B — first 2-3 interviews feel awkward, you catch yourself pitching:** Normal. Score honestly (Mia scored her first call 3/10). Recover on calls 4-10. The skill is in the recovery.
- **🆘 Plan B — interviewee has no story (genuine blankness, not evasion):** They don't have the problem. End call politely at 10 minutes. This IS data about your `[customer]` blank.
- **🆘 Plan B — booked 10 interviews but fewer than 7 show up:** Book 12-15 expecting ~30% no-show. If only 4 show, the ICP targeting or outreach message is wrong — re-check your 2.3 community list and 2.4 message before re-booking. Research panel fallback (UserInterviews, $25-$50/screener) guarantees show rate.

### 2.5 · Mom Test Synthesis: Build, Pivot, or Kill
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 90 minutes with the 10 transcripts, a printed template, pen.
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** 10 scored Mom Test transcripts
- **📝 Template:** Validated Problem Statement — 5 sections: who has the problem, what it costs them, what they've tried, why now, pain magnitude
- **📝 Gate:** Score each interview 1-10, count strong signals (7+). ≥7 = BUILD, 4-6 = PIVOT, <4 = KILL
- **📦 Output:** Build/pivot/kill decision + one-page Validated Problem Statement
- **🆘 Plan B — pivot (4-6 strong signals):** ICP problem, not problem problem. Sharpen ICP definition, run 5 more interviews against narrower group. Cost: 1 week.
- **🆘 Plan B — kill (<4 strong signals):** Strangers were polite. Return to 1.1, rewrite weakest blank from dead transcripts, re-run 2.3.
- **🆘 Plan B — numbers won't settle (call feels both 7 and 5):** Scoring Q4 alone without comparison test and flag count. Re-read 2.1 scoring rubric — 7 needs comparison + 3+ emotional flags or rounds to 5.

### 2.6 · Build a Clickable Prototype
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 2 hours build + 5 × 30-minute observation sessions.
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Lovable free tier)
- **📦 Input:** BUILD decision + validated problem statement + 5 strongest-signal interviewees (scored ≥7)
- **🔑 Setup:** Lovable account (sign up at lovable.dev)
- **📝 Template:** 3-screen prototype prompt — fill `[PRODUCT CATEGORY]`, `[customer]`, `[PRIMARY ACTION]`, `[FAKE DATA]`, `[BUTTON LABEL]`
- **📝 Gate:** 4+ of 5 sessions pass (reaches Screen 3 without coaching, describes product accurately, first 3 clicks correct)
- **📦 Output:** 5 silent-observation sessions scored + verbatim vocabulary from closing question
- **🆘 Plan B — 2-3 passes:** Revise one element (vocabulary, Screen 1 layout, or CTA label), run 2 replacement sessions. One iteration only.
- **🆘 Plan B — 0-1 pass:** Solution shape wrong. Return to 2.1 before writing any brief. Shape mismatch = interview signal was off.
- **🆘 Plan B — can't get a navigable prototype in 10 Lovable exchanges:** Hypothesis or prompt is too vague. Sharpen before continuing. Do NOT brute-force with more messages.

## MODULE 3: Design from Evidence (~1 week)

### 3.1 · One-Page Product Brief (Vibe PRD)
- **⏳ Wait:** None. One focused sitting (after a night's sleep between drafts).
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** Validated problem statement (2.5) + verbatim prototype vocabulary (2.6)
- **📝 Template:** Vibe PRD — 5 sections: Problem (copied verbatim from 2.5), User & context (60s before/after), What you're building (one paragraph, verb-led), Success metric (one number/unit/timeframe), What you're NOT building (5-8 no-go bullets)
- **📝 Constraint:** ≤250 words, one side of paper
- **📦 Output:** One-page Product Brief in `Founder OS` folder
- **🆘 Plan B — can't fit Section 3 in one paragraph:** Scope is too big. Pick the single smallest workflow one persona can complete end-to-end. Cut everything else to no-go list in Section 5.
- **🆘 Plan B — brief keeps growing beyond one page:** If a feature in Section 3 doesn't trace back to a verbatim Module 2 interview quote, cut it. Can't name which interviewee said they needed it? It's your feature, not theirs.

### 3.2 · Quality-Check: Features to Outcomes
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 20-minute rewrite + peer/AI review.
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Claude/ChatGPT free tier for AI reviewer if no peer available)
- **📦 Input:** One-page Product Brief from 3.1
- **📝 Template:** Rewrite every feature noun in Section 3 as *When / I want / So I can* job stories
- **📝 Gate:** Peer (or Claude/ChatGPT) names nothing outside your Section 3 scope and no-go list. Brief FAILS if their answer includes 2+ items outside.
- **📦 Output:** Outcome-shaped Section 3 + quality-check verdict
- **🆘 Plan B — reviewer names 2+ things outside no-go list:** Section 3 is still vague — names software parts, not moments and results. Tighten the *When/I want/So I can* shape on the loosest line and ask a fresh reviewer.

## MODULE 4: Build It Yourself (2-4 weeks)

### 4.1 · Should You Hire? The 2026 Decision Tree
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 20-minute solo exercise + 20-minute peer sanity check.
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** Quality-checked one-page brief (3.2)
- **📝 Template:** Build Path Decision Worksheet — 5 yes/no questions
- **📦 Output:** 4-way build-path decision (self-serve / validate / fractional CTO / hire)
- **🆘 Plan B — matrix routes to "hire" but runway <4 months:** Runway wins. Default to self-serve (4.3-4.4), ship smallest validated core, revisit hiring when 4.5 ceiling signal fires.
- **🆘 Plan B — validate-without-code path (Airbnb test):** Carrd page + Stripe checkout + Notion FAQ → send to 30-name list from 2.3. 2 paying buyers from 30 = build switch flipped. 0 clicks = pitch/price/timing wrong. Find out for $200 instead of $30K.

### 4.2 · Ownership Audit
- **⏳ Wait:** Domain transfer: up to 14 days (registrar transfer + ICANN). GitHub org transfer: 7 days if contractor cooperates. AWS root recovery: 3-5 business days if email is lost.
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (if accounts are already in your name). Domain transfer: ~$10/year. AWS: no cost to own.
- **📦 Input:** Build-path decision from 4.1. Applies to ALL paths.
- **🔑 Setup:** GitHub account + AWS account + domain registrar account — all under founder's company email
- **📝 Template:** Ownership Checklist — 4 zones: Code (GitHub org Owner), Cloud (AWS root email + MFA), Secrets (database passwords accessible solo), Domain (WHOIS shows you)
- **📦 Output:** All infrastructure accounts confirmed in founder's name
- **🆘 Plan B — contractor won't transfer GitHub org:** Retain a lawyer. $2K-$5K beats a stalled checkout. Run the Ownership Audit Flow diagram for the full escalation path.
- **🆘 Plan B — domain in page builder's name from Module 1:** Transfer to a registrar in your own name (Porkbun ~$10/yr). Point it nowhere until deploy.
- **🆘 Plan B — can't reach the person with AWS root access:** AWS account recovery process — 3-5 business days, requires ID verification.

### 4.3 · Self-Serve Stack: Tools & Setup
- **⏳ Wait:** None (Lovable, Supabase signups are instant). GitHub sync activation: immediate.
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (all three tools have free tiers)
- **📦 Input:** "Self-serve" build decision (4.1) + quality-checked brief (3.2) + passed ownership audit (4.2)
- **🔑 Setup:** Lovable account + Supabase account + Stripe already set up from 1.5. GitHub sync in Lovable Settings on Day 1.
- **📝 Template:** 3 pre-flight rules: (1) brief passed 3.2 quality-gate, (2) one workflow/one persona/one happy path in one sentence, (3) GitHub sync ON
- **📝 Pre-build check:** Paste Section 3 into Claude with leakage-check prompt. Save 3 flagged features as scope-leak watchlist.
- **📦 Output:** 3 pre-flight rules locked + scope-leak watchlist + tool boundaries clear
- **🆘 Plan B — 12-rules list feels overwhelming:** Only rules 1, 2, and 6 (the 3 pre-flight rules) must be true before starting. Other 9 fire inline during 4.4 build.
- **🆘 Plan B — Lovable drops work, no GitHub sync:** Lesson 4.4 demands GitHub sync on Day 1 as pre-flight rule 3. Without it, source is unrecoverable after subscription cancellation.

### 4.4 · Self-Serve Stack: Build Phases
- **⏳ Wait:** Build time: 2-4 weeks. Phase 1 (UI): 1-2 sessions. Phase 2 (auth): 2-4 sessions. Phase 3 (Stripe): 3-5 sessions. Phase 4 (deploy): 2-4 sessions.
- **💰 Cost:** Lovable + Supabase entry paid tiers + domain (~$10/yr) + Stripe per-transaction fees
- **📦 Input:** Self-serve stack set up (4.3) + one-page brief from Module 3
- **📝 Template:** 4 build phases: Phase 1 UI → Phase 2 Supabase+auth → Phase 3 Stripe+checkout → Phase 4 staging URL+5 ICP users. Each phase ends with one demo to one human.

- **📦 Output:** Live MVP at real URL — all accounts in founder's name
- **🆘 Plan B — demo person needs a tour to understand the screen:** Design carries meaning that should be on screen. Rewrite Lovable prompt around the one job the screen does. If 2 testers stall on first screen, workflow shape is wrong — revisit 3.2 outcomes before adding Stripe.
- **🆘 Plan B — red light: Console errors on signup+checkout:** Fix before advancing. Red gate hands Module 5 an MVP real users can't touch.

### 4.5 · Ceiling Signals (OPTIONAL monthly check)

## MODULE 5: First Paying Customer (2-4 weeks)

### 5.1 · Sean Ellis 40% Test
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 5 questions, Typeform/Google Form takes 2 minutes to set up. Send to earliest users, results within 24 hours.
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Typeform/Google Form free tier)
- **📦 Input:** ≥10-30 users on your MVP (from 4.4 onramp + 2.3-2.4 interviewees)
- **📝 Template:** 5 questions verbatim in lesson 5.1. Q1: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?" Q2-Q4: open-ended "why" + "what would you use instead" + "primary benefit". Q5: segment question (role/use case).
- **📝 Gate:** 40%+ answer "very disappointed" in at least one segment
- **📦 Output:** Must-have segment identified + per-segment must-have %
- **🆘 Plan B — under 40% overall but one segment is near:** Isolate by Q5 segment column. Pivot the Q1 column. If one segment is at 40%+ while others are low, you have found your must-have segment.
- **🆘 Plan B — no segment above 25%:** Product problem, not marketing problem. Fix the product before acquiring. Back to 4.4 — ship against the most common Q3 verbatim answer.

### 5.2 · Channel Selection (OPTIONAL)
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 30-minute solo exercise.
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** Verbatim answers from 5.1 Q2-Q3
- **📝 Template:** Score candidate channels out of 12. Pick one. Commit to one full send-reply-follow-up arc before switching.
- **📦 Output:** One committed channel
- **🆘 Plan B — skip entirely:** If your channel is already obvious (personal network for first 10), skip to 5.3.

### 5.3 · Build Your 50-Name Network List
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 30-60 minutes to populate spreadsheet.
- **💰 Cost:** $0
- **📦 Input:** Must-have segment identified (5.1)
- **🔑 Setup:** Google Sheet in `Founder OS` folder
- **📝 Template:** 6 columns: Name, Company, Role, Bucket (Champions/Hot/Warm/Cold), Relationship strength, Last contact date. Seed with 2.4 interviewees + 1.4 waitlist signups + 2.3 30-name list.
- **📦 Output:** 50 names sorted into 4 outreach buckets
- **🆘 Plan B — personal network has only 5 names:** Five is enough to start. Message all five before cold outbound. If truly zero applicable names, skip to 5.7 cold outbound.

### 5.4 · Write the Outreach Message
- **⏳ Wait:** None. 90 seconds to record Loom.
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (Loom free tier)
- **📦 Input:** 50-name list sorted into 4 buckets (5.3)
- **🔑 Setup:** Loom account
- **📝 Template:** 4-part message structure — (1) Bucket-specific opener, (2) One line on the problem in their language (use 5.1 Q3 verbatims), (3) 90-second Loom (60s product, 30s you), (4) Specific ask + Calendly link + "paid pilot" teaser
- **📦 Output:** 4 message variants + 90-second Loom recording
- **🆘 Plan B — message reads generic after 2 rewrites:** Using marketing voice not verbatim quotes. Paste exact Q3 answers from 5.1 survey into Part 2.
- **🆘 Plan B — can't find a real reference for cold-bucket name:** Move them to 5.7 cold outbound. The playbook there is designed for zero-context outreach.

### 5.5 · Send, Track, and Read the Replies
- **⏳ Wait:** Reply tail can run 1-2 weeks
- **💰 Cost:** $0

- **🔑 Setup:** Same spreadsheet from 5.3. Calendly for bookings.
- **📝 Template:** Send sequence: champions Monday → hot Tuesday → warm Wednesday → cold Thursday. Track: Reply, Demo booked, DPA sent, Deposit cleared columns.
- **📦 Output:** Demos booked from warm list
- **🆘 Plan B — reply rate under 5% after full send:** Message framing is wrong, not the list. Re-record Loom using 5.1 Q2-Q3 verbatims as script. Re-send to non-responders with "re-recorded this" subject.
- **🆘 Plan B — lots of replies but no demos booked:** Calendly link is missing or buried. Make it the most prominent element in the message.

### 5.6 · Charge Before You Ship: The Paid Pilot
- **⏳ Wait:** DPA legal review: 2-3 business days if customer's legal reviews it (most one-page DPAs clear without legal)
- **💰 Cost:** $0 (DPA template free). Stripe per-transaction fees on deposit.
- **📦 Input:** 3-5 warm leads with demos booked from 5.5
- **🔑 Setup:** Stripe already from 1.5. DocuSign/HelloSign or PDF+email for signature.
- **📝 Template:** One-page Design Partner Agreement — 6 sections: (1) Scope of pilot (3 outcomes, 2 use cases), (2) Duration+dates (6-8 weeks, weekly Friday demo), (3) Pilot fee+deposit (10-30% year-one ACV, **$500 floor**, via Stripe before kickoff), (4) Success criteria (3 measurable, 2 of 3 = auto-convert), (5) Conversion terms, (6) Data/IP/termination
- **📝 Deposit math:** 1.5 price × 12 = year-one ACV. Deposit = 10-30%. When 10% of ACV < $300: charge first month upfront instead. Low-price path in 5.6.
- **📝 Gate:** Stripe deposit cleared ≥$500. That is the "first paying customer."
- **📦 Output:** Signed DPA + cleared deposit
- **🆘 Plan B — "can we start free and convert later?":** Reframe deposit as year-one ACV prepaid, not added cost. $500 sits in this month's accounting and gains CFO check now; free pilots lose that approval in Week 8.
- **🆘 Plan B — "we do not do paid pilots":** Wrong buyer profile — not in your must-have segment. Thank them, move to next warm lead.
- **🆘 Plan B — legal review taking >1 week:** Send kickoff prep doc while waiting. Most one-page DPAs clear without legal review. If they insist, offer a DocuSign-importable version from the operating kit.

- **🔑 Setup:** Apollo free tier (for contact enrichment) or manual LinkedIn search
- **📝 Template:** 3 sector-specific scripts (B2B SaaS, B2B services, B2C app) + First 10 Customers Google Sheet tracker

- **🆘 Plan B — 0 replies from 30 messages:** The 4-line script is wrong or the ICP filter is wrong. Tighten one, re-send 20.
- **🆘 Plan B — Apollo credits exhausted on free tier:** Enrich by hand in the Google Sheet. At this volume, manual enrichment is bottlenecked by your time more than your budget.
