# Customer Profile + Four Forces - "Alex", the burned non-technical founder

**Created**: 2026-07-21 | **Owner**: agent-built; Paul reviews | **Frameworks**: JTBD Customer Profile + the Four Forces of Progress
**Why**: the assumptions register (A0) votes on WHO/WHERE; this profile explains WHY they switch - the demand model underneath the campaign. Push+Pull generate demand, Anxiety+Habit block it. Messaging amplifies the first pair and dismantles the second.
**Sources**: castandhue.com/post/four-forces-progress (Steve Koch, 2024); jobstobedone.org/the-four-forces; Moesta, *Demand-Side Sales*. ICP base: `90.10`.

## 1. The Customer (one real person, not a segment)

**Alex** - non-technical founder, pre-seed to Series A ($100K-$2M raised, $5-30K/mo dev budget), decides alone under $50K. Paid a dev shop, a freelancer, or an AI tool (Lovable / Cursor / Bolt / Replit) to build the product. The demo landed. Real users arrived. Now things break and Alex can't tell whether one more round of fixes will help or just burn another month - and can't tell whether the developers are being straight.

## 2. The Job to be Done

- **Functional job**: "Get my product working reliably for real users, and get back control of the code I paid for."
- **Emotional job**: "Stop feeling stupid and lied to. Stop lying awake wondering if the whole thing is going to fall over."
- **Social job**: "Look like a founder in control in front of my investors, my team, and my users - not someone who got taken."

**The struggling moment** (when the job goes from passive to active): the app breaks in front of a real user or an investor, OR the founder realizes they can't get the code / can't evaluate the next $25K quote. That is the buying window.

**Timeline stage to catch them**: *active looking* - they are already searching "fix my app," asking a peer, or posting for help. Our free audit meets them exactly there.

## 3. The Four Forces acting on Alex

### PUSH - of the situation (drives change) - AMPLIFY
The pain making the status quo unbearable. **This is what we LISTEN for to find them** (= the trigger taxonomy).
- The app worked in the demo and now breaks for real users (checkout fails, data corrupts, sessions drop).
- Alex can't get the code / it lives in the shop's GitHub / the server's on the shop's account.
- Developers keep saying "refactoring" / "architecture" and no working product ships.
- About to sign another $25K+ contract and has no way to judge it.
- A security scare (leaked data, a stolen key) or an investor-diligence question they couldn't answer.
> **Real founder language** (harvest these verbatim for messaging): "it worked yesterday," "I don't know what I actually have," "they won't give me access," "is this normal?", "I can't tell if they're making progress."

### PULL - of the new solution (drives change) - AMPLIFY
What makes JetThoughts attractive. Our differentiators, not the generic "free audit" six competitors offer.
- A senior engineer reads YOUR actual code and tells you the truth in plain English - free, no contract.
- Fixed price, agreed up front ($25-50K) so the number can't creep.
- **You own the code and every account at each milestone** - control handed back, not held.
- Paul sits on every call as your fractional CTO and translates what the developers are doing into decisions you can make.

### ANXIETY - of the new solution (blocks change) - DISMANTLE
The fears that stop Alex saying yes even with strong push+pull. **These are the objections T9 must defuse, with proof.**
- "I got burned once - why won't this be another agency that overpromises?" → free audit first, zero contract; you judge us before paying anything.
- "Is $25K real, or does it balloon like last time?" → fixed price agreed up front, in writing.
- "Will I understand any of it, or be in the dark again?" → plain-English, Paul on every call, ownership at each milestone.
- "Can they even work on my code if the shop holds it?" → the audit surfaces this first (repo-access intake question); we help you get access.
- "What if it's not salvageable and I wasted more money?" → we tell you salvage-vs-rebuild honestly, even when rebuild is the harder sale for us.
> Dismantle with: the free audit (risk reversal), fixed price (certainty), milestone ownership (control), 4.8/5 from 32 clients + 3-year average relationships (proof), salvage-vs-rebuild honesty (trust).

### HABIT - of the present (blocks change) - DISMANTLE
The inertia keeping Alex stuck with the current shop / doing nothing.
- "One more sprint will probably fix it" (sunk cost + hope).
- Switching feels like starting over / losing the money already spent.
- The current shop is a known quantity; a new team is unknown risk.
- No time - firefighting daily leaves no room to evaluate alternatives.
> Break with: reframe "one more sprint" as the trap (the audit gives a straight salvage-vs-rebuild answer in 45 min, not another month); "you keep 30-50% of what you have, you're not throwing it away"; the audit costs nothing and one call, so switching-to-look is near-zero friction.

## 5. How we FIND them - forces → channel → message/post/comment (Paul's question)

Each Push signal is observable somewhere. Listen for the push, respond with the pull, pre-empt the anxiety.

| Push signal (listen for) | Where it shows up (channel) | What we do there | Message / post / comment shape |
|---|---|---|---|
| "app broke after launch / at scale" | r/vibecoding, r/SaaS, r/startups (excerpts), IndieHackers, X, competitor "free audit" comment threads | reply in-thread as a helper, not a pitch | **Comment**: name the specific failure they described, offer the free audit read - "Happy to have one of our senior engineers look at the actual code and send you a plain-English read on what's breaking and whether it's salvageable. No pitch." |
| "dev shop holds my code / no access" | r/startups, r/smallbusiness, LinkedIn | warm-intro or careful DM | **DM**: lead with ownership - "You should own the code you paid for. We can audit what you have and help you get access + a straight salvage-vs-rebuild read." |
| "about to sign a $25K contract, can't judge it" | LinkedIn, founder Slack/Discord | Paul's LinkedIn (manual) | **Message**: "Before you sign, one senior engineer will read the codebase free and tell you if the quote matches reality." |
| "first eng hire / CTO left" | LinkedIn hiring posts, Wellfound, r/cto | warm-intro / LinkedIn | **Message**: position the audit as the senior read before they over-hire. |
| "we need to refactor" confusion | r/startups, r/Entrepreneur | reply with a helpful JT post, then audit | **Comment**: educate first (link the relevant blog post), then offer the audit. |
| real-time break ("database wiped", "login broke") | Lovable/Bolt/Replit #help Discords, Cursor forum (Paul-manual join) | join, help in-channel | **Reply**: helper-first; move to DM only if they ask. |

**Content angle (posts we publish, not just replies)**: write to the push and the anxiety - "Your MVP looks finished but keeps breaking" (push), "You own the code you paid for" (anxiety/habit). The 5 Control posts (runbook C2) map to push signals 1-5.

**Proof-of-pain anchor for all messaging**: Jason Lemkin / Replit DB-wipe ("I destroyed months of work in seconds") - the ICP's worst fear on record; reference it to make the push visceral without fear-mongering.

**Guardrail**: never open cold with the pitch. The six competitor rescue shops all lead with "free audit" - we lead with the founder's own words (push) + the ownership/truth wedge (pull), and pre-empt "another agency will burn me" (anxiety) in the first two lines. Community rules punish self-promo; be the helper who happens to run rescues.
