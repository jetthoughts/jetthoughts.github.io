## 📅 10-WEEK PUBLISHING SCHEDULE (Apr 10 – Jun 20, 2026)

### Frequency: 2-3 posts/week (target 10/month)

**Why 2-3/week:** HubSpot benchmarks show B2B companies publishing 11+ posts/month get ~3x more traffic than those at 1-3/month. Semrush data puts the sweet spot at 8-16/month. thoughtbot publishes 3-5/week. For a small team (2-3 people), 10 posts/month is sustainable without quality dilution.

**Mix:** ~6 long-form SEO pieces/month (1,200-2,000 words) + ~4 shorter experience/process posts (600-1,000 words). Long-form drives organic search; short-form feeds social and newsletter.

## 🎯 AUDIENCE TARGETING (apply BEFORE writing)

Before drafting any post, decide which audience the topic actually serves:

| Audience | Post types | Voice | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| **🧑‍💼 ICP-E founders** | Hire/fire/evaluate decisions, transparency, contracts, dev-shop pain | Walling-at-a-bar, plain English, empathy-first, founder-to-founder | 1200-1800 words |
| **💎 Rails developers / CTOs** | CVE patches, framework upgrades, deployment, performance, technical mechanics | thoughtbot-style technical writing, code blocks, citations, terse | 600-1000 words |
| **🤖 Crossover** | News-pegged AI incidents with policy/decision implications | Mixes both depending on framing | varies |

**Rule of thumb**: when a post *requires* technical detail to be useful (gadget chain mechanics, detection commands, upgrade paths), it's developer-targeted. Strip the "your dev shop didn't tell you" hooks, drop the 90-minute founder checklist, drop the rescue-engagement marketing narrative. The CTA should be light or absent — technical authority is the goal, not lead-gen.

When a post helps a non-technical reader make a non-technical decision (whether to switch shops, what to ask before signing, how to read a weekly report), it's founder-targeted. Use the voice guide below.

When in doubt: read the brief and ask "could a developer get value from this without the founder framing layer?" If yes, drop the founder framing. Crossover posts (e.g. PocketOS — incident affected non-technical founders directly) only stay crossover when both audiences need the post's specific framing.

## 🎨 VOICE & STRUCTURE GUIDE (ICP-E Founder Content)

### Tone
- **Empathy first** — acknowledge the pain before offering the solution
- **Plain English** — zero jargon; if you must use a technical term, explain it in parentheses
- **Founder-to-founder** — write like a founder talking to a founder, not a vendor pitching
- **Specific over vague** — "$12K planned, $12.8K actual (+6.7%)" beats "on budget"
- **No generic AI language** — "unlock", "harness", "leverage", "dive in" are BANNED

### Structure
1. **Punchy hook** — 1-2 sentences, often reframing a common pain
2. **Primary keyword in first 100 words** (SEO requirement)
3. **Numbered sections** with action-oriented titles ("5 Signs It's Time to Fire" not "Overview")
4. **Founder perspective** — "As a non-technical founder, you can't code-review, but you CAN..."
5. **Checklist or decision tree** (high conversion, shareable)
6. **JetThoughts proof point** — one paragraph showing how we handle this (transparency, not sales pitch)
7. **CTA** — specific next step, not generic "contact us"
8. **Related reading** — 2-3 internal links in prose
9. **Further reading** — 3-5 external citations (Indie Hackers, YC, Forrester, Reddit)
