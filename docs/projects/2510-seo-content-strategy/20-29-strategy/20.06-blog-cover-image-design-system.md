## 🎯 Design Intent In One Paragraph

Every jt_site cover image is a **minimalist hand-drawn editorial sketch** rendered in charcoal on cream paper, built around a single visual metaphor for the post's concept. No text, no logos, no UI chrome, no tech clichés (code screens, gears, lightbulbs, robots). The aesthetic reads as confident editorial illustration — closer to a New Yorker spot illustration than a stock tech photo. The Hugo theme overlays the post title separately, so the image itself is pure imagery.

---

## 📊 Research Basis (2025–2026)

Three evidence lines converged on this direction:

### 1. Engagement data

- **High-contrast designs perform ~20% better** than low-contrast ([Fastercapital](https://fastercapital.com/content/Reddit-post-thumbnail--The-Psychology-Behind-Effective-Reddit-Post-Thumbnails-for-Business-Success.html))
- **Custom thumbnails can lift CTR by up to 30%**, with some reports showing 150% engagement lift vs generic imagery ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/why-your-thumbnails-matter-look-data-behind-user-meer-shah--lpjjf))
- **Curved shapes enhance click-through rates** significantly ([Journal of Consumer Research](https://academic.oup.com/jcr/article/51/3/552/7470747))
- **Single-focal-point minimalist designs outperform cluttered designs** ([Contabo](https://contabo.com/blog/minimalist-web-design-trends-examples/))

### 2. 2025 visual trends

- **Hand-drawn sketches and line art are the dominant editorial trend**, specifically because they read as human in contrast to overly-digital AI output ([getfuelpod](https://www.getfuelpod.com/post/10-hand-drawn-art-trends-for-pod-in-2025))
- **Minimalism with 60-70% negative space** has replaced information-dense thumbnails as the engagement winner ([Contabo](https://contabo.com/blog/minimalist-web-design-trends-examples/))
- **Slight hand-drawn imperfections** create emotional connection and authenticity ([Scan2CAD](https://www.scan2cad.com/blog/cad/hand-drawings/))

### 3. AI prompt engineering best practices

- Structure prompts as `[Subject] + [Style] + [Approach] + [Context] + [Composition] + [Colors] + [Quality]` ([Cursor-IDE](https://www.cursor-ide.com/blog/chatgpt-image-prompts-guide-2025))
- Use **negative prompts** to exclude clutter explicitly ([OpenAI Community](https://community.openai.com/t/dalle3-prompt-to-generate-pencil-sketches-keeps-including-pencils-in-image/678372))
- **Iterate on one variable at a time** rather than batch-tweaking ([Let's Enhance](https://letsenhance.io/blog/article/ai-text-prompt-guide/))

## 🎨 The Design System

### 1. Composition

| Rule | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | **1200×630 px** (landscape) | Open Graph + Twitter card standard; renders correctly across LinkedIn, X, Slack, Discord |
| Focal point | **Single hero element** | Cluttered thumbnails underperform; one metaphor per image |
| Placement | Rule of thirds or centered | Standard editorial composition |
| Negative space | **60–70%** of total canvas | Breathing room is the #1 engagement lift in 2025 |
| Minimum margin | **15% on all sides** | Leaves room for the theme's title overlay without collision |
| Borders/frames | **None** | The theme may crop; borders would be clipped |

### 2. Style

| Rule | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Technique | **Hand-drawn digital sketch** | Trend winner for 2025; distinguishes from AI-generic output |
| Stroke weight | **Consistent 2–3 pt** | Reads as editorial, not CAD-diagram |
| Line quality | Slight hand-drawn imperfections | Authenticity signal; prevents robotic AI look |
| Texture | Subtle paper / canvas grain | Depth without visual noise |
| Digital effects | None (no gradients, glows, 3D, neon) | Clashes with editorial aesthetic |

### 3. Color palette

| Role | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Background | **`#FFF9F0`** (cream) or **`#F9F9F9`** (near-white) | Softer than pure white, premium editorial feel, reduces eye strain |
| Line art | **`#333333`** (charcoal) | Softer than pure black; readable but not harsh |
| Accent | **None by default** | Pure monochrome is cleaner and scales across all topics |
| Optional accent | Single bold color, ≤10% of pixels | For flagship/anniversary posts only; current wave does not use accents |

Reserved named accents for future use (not wired in yet):
- **Signal Orange** — urgency / breaking change posts
- **Soviet Red** — opinion / hot-take posts
- **Deep Blue** — deep-dive / reference posts

### 4. Content rules (the metaphor rule)

The hardest rule to encode, the one that matters most: **the hero element must be a metaphor, not a literal depiction.**

- ❌ DON'T draw: code, screens, terminals, keyboards, editors, language logos, robots, gears, lightbulbs, brains, clouds-with-servers
- ✅ DO draw: physical, natural, or architectural objects that capture the IDEA behind the post

Reference translations (for intuition, not as a lookup table):

| Post concept | Metaphorical translation |
|---|---|
| Pausing and resuming long jobs | A flowing river interrupted by stepping stones |
| Replacing one backend with another | A bridge being rebuilt from different material while traffic still crosses |
| Structured logging | Loose papers being sorted into labeled drawers |
| HTTP/2 compression | A wide road narrowing into an efficient channel |
| Test-driven development | Scaffolding erected before a building |

### 5. Absolute prohibitions

These are enforced in the script's prompt as negative directives:

- **No text of any kind** — letters, words, numbers, watermarks, signatures. The Hugo theme overlays the post title separately; any text in the image would double-print and look broken.
- **No logos** — neither tech brand marks (Rails, Ruby, Docker) nor JetThoughts branding. Logos are cliché and tie the image to a specific tech rather than the concept.
- **No UI chrome** — browser windows, buttons, modals, nav bars. This is editorial illustration, not a product screenshot.
- **No people's faces** — recognizable portraits date fast and create rights complications.
- **No digital effects** — neon, rainbow gradients, glow, 3D renders all fight the hand-drawn aesthetic.

## 🔗 Mapping Research → Script

The script `bin/generate-cover-image` bakes this entire design system into a single enhanced prompt. Mapping:

| Design rule | Where it lives in the prompt |
|---|---|
| 1200×630 aspect ratio | `STYLE` line + `OUTPUT` line |
| Charcoal on cream | `STYLE: Minimalist hand-drawn digital sketch. Consistent 2-3pt charcoal (#333333) line art on cream background (#FFF9F0).` |
| 60-70% negative space | `COMPOSITION: ... 60-70% negative space, minimum 15% margin on all sides.` |
| Single focal point | `COMPOSITION: Single focal point ... One hero element only.` |
| Hand-drawn imperfections | `STYLE: ... Slight hand-drawn imperfections — feels sketched in a notebook, not a CAD diagram.` |
| Metaphor rule | `CONTENT: Translate the concept below into a physical, natural, or architectural VISUAL METAPHOR — not a literal depiction.` |
| Anti-cliché list | `CONTENT: Do not draw code, screens, terminals, keyboards, language logos, robots, gears, or lightbulbs (cliches).` |
| Absolute prohibitions | `PROHIBITIONS: No text, letters, numbers, or watermarks ... No logos. No UI chrome. No neon or 3D.` |

The final prompt is ~1,180 characters — tight enough for any image model's context window, explicit enough that different invocations produce visually consistent output.

## 🧭 How to Use This System

### For writing a new post

1. Draft the post's `index.md` normally per the wave plan's Voice & Structure Guide.
2. Before committing, distill the post into **one concept sentence** for the cover. This should describe the IDEA, not the topic. Examples:
   - ✅ "pausing and resuming a long computation"
   - ✅ "rebuilding an engine mid-flight"
   - ❌ "Rails 8.1 Active Job Continuations" (too literal — AI will draw a code editor)
3. Run:
   ```bash
   bin/generate-cover-image --slug <your-post-slug> "your concept sentence"
   ```
4. Inspect the output. If it drew a cliché anyway (code/gears/lightbulbs), rephrase the concept more abstractly and re-run.
5. Commit the post + `cover.jpg` together.

### For iterating on the design system itself

If you find a rule that consistently produces bad output, update THIS doc first (state the rule and the reasoning), then update the script's prompt template to match. Keep the script and the doc in sync — if the script has a rule the doc doesn't explain, the rule is undocumented.

## 🚫 What Was Intentionally Left Out (YAGNI discipline)

These were in the research but deliberately NOT wired into the script:

| Element | Why omitted |
|---|---|
| `--accent` flag | We're running pure monochrome for the entire current wave; no accent needed until a flagship post demands one. Add the flag when the first real use case appears. |
| "Agitator poster" headline text inside the image | The Hugo theme overlays its own title; baking a second headline in the image would fight it. |
| Border / double-line frame | The theme may crop images; borders would clip. |
| Halftone shading | Adds noise without clear engagement benefit; easy to re-add later if needed. |
| Platform-specific modifiers (LinkedIn / X / Facebook) | We publish to the site, not directly to platforms. OG tags handle platform rendering. |
| Style variations (blueprint / notebook / whiteboard) | Multiple styles kill visual consistency across the blog; pick one and stay. |
| Multiple concept-specific prompt templates (programming / AI / cloud / security) | Over-fitting. A single well-constructed metaphor directive works across all topics. |

If future evidence proves any of these omissions wrong, update this doc's "what was left out" table and the script's prompt together.

## 🔬 Known Limitations and Open Questions

1. **Image generation requires a direct Gemini API key — OAuth auth is not enough.** The gemini CLI defaults to OAuth personal login, which routes to `cloudcode-pa.googleapis.com` (Google's Code Assist endpoint). Verified on 2026-04-09: **that endpoint returns 404 for both `gemini-2.5-flash-image` and `gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview`** — image generation models are not published on the Code Assist auth path. To use image models, export `GEMINI_API_KEY` from [Google AI Studio](https://aistudio.google.com/app/apikey); gemini CLI will then route to `generativelanguage.googleapis.com`, where Nano Banana and Nano Banana 2 are accessible. The script's `fail_with_log()` helper detects the 404 pattern and surfaces this remediation automatically.
2. **Metaphor quality depends on prompt framing.** If the user passes a topic ("Rails 8.1 features") instead of a concept ("rebuilding an engine mid-flight"), the model will likely default to tech clichés regardless of prohibitions. The metaphor rule requires user discipline at the call site.
3. **Consistency across posts is unproven.** With one post in the wave so far, we haven't validated that the same prompt template produces visually consistent output across 5 different topics. Run a batch and inspect side-by-side after wave 1 finishes.
4. **gemini CLI version drift.** The default model `gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview` is a preview SKU released 2026-02-26. Google may rename, GA, or deprecate it; check [Google's image generation docs](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/image-generation) if the 404 persists even with a valid `GEMINI_API_KEY`.

### Trend and Engagement Data (2025)

Minimalist designs with hand-drawn elements are dominating 2025. Studies show that thumbnails can increase click-through rates by up to 30%, with some reports indicating custom thumbnails lead to 150% engagement increases. Specifically for technical content, hand-drawn sketches and line art are trending heavily, as they provide a human touch that contrasts with overly digital aesthetics.

- High-contrast designs perform 20% better
- Curved shapes enhance click-through rates significantly
- Minimalist approaches with single focal points outperform cluttered designs
- Hand-drawn elements create emotional connections that drive engagement

### The Perfect AI Prompt Structure

Core Formula: `[Subject/Concept] + [Hand-drawn Style] + [Minimalist Approach] + [Technical Context] + [Composition] + [Color Scheme] + [Quality Parameters]`

Detailed Prompt Template:
```
Create a minimalist hand-drawn sketch style illustration of [TECHNICAL CONCEPT],
featuring clean line art with subtle geometric elements, single focal point composition,
[SPECIFIC TECH ELEMENT] rendered in simple black ink strokes on white background,
technical diagram aesthetic with annotation-style details,
modern minimalist design perfect for social media thumbnail,
high contrast, crisp lines, professional sketch style,
16:9 aspect ratio, clean and uncluttered
```

### Style Specifications for Maximum Engagement

- Line weight: Consistent 2-3px stroke width
- Color palette: Monochromatic black on white, or single accent color
- Composition: Single focal point, rule of thirds
- Whitespace: 60-70% negative space for breathing room
- Hand-drawn feel: Slight imperfections to convey authenticity
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 for optimal social media display
- Resolution: 1280x720 minimum for clarity
- Safe zones: Keep text/logos in center 80% area

### JetThoughts "Technical Narrative" Design Language

**Visual Composition**
- Aspect Ratio: 1:1 square for social versatility, or 1200×630 for SEO
- Three-tier vertical stack: top heading, center focal illustration, bottom subtext
- 15-20% margin around all elements

**Style & Iconography**
- Hand-drawn digital sketches or clean vector blueprint styles; no realistic photos
- Metaphorical storytelling — show the problem or action, not the logo
- Consistent medium-weight strokes (2-4pt)
- Subtle paper or canvas grain texture

**Color Palette**
- Background: off-white, light cream, or very light gray (`#F9F9F9` or `#FFF9F0`)
- Primary action color: one bold accent (Soviet Red, Signal Orange, Deep Blue)
- Neutral secondary: dark charcoal (`#333333`) instead of pure black

**Typography** (for layouts that include in-image text — **not used by jt_site**)
- Strong high-contrast sans-serif (Montserrat, Inter, Bebas Neue)
- All-caps for main headline
- Headline ≥3x larger than any other text

**Agitator Twist** (optional USA/USSR poster style)
- Thin double-line border or wood-block frame
- Subtle halftone dot patterns for shading
- "Action-first" copy

**Prompt Formula:**
> Minimalist hand-sketched digital illustration, [TOPIC] theme, [ACCENT COLOR] and charcoal on cream paper background. USA/USSR agitator poster style. Bold blocky sans-serif typography at top reading "[HEADLINE]". Center focus: single metaphorical object [OBJECT]. Clean composition, wide margins, high contrast, textured paper feel. No clutter.
